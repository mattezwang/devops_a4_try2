reflection

What interested me was how the build loops in both parts really converged after the first iteration, even though I was expecting it to converge after 6-8 iterations. This demonstrated how important the review and planning staged before coding were. Both the planning documents had pseudocode for how I would approach the logic rather than just simple descirptions, which allowed for less room for error, as there were pretty much no design desicions to make for the building agent. This is the "loop" in the Ralph Wiggum workflow, as I'd assumed it would be self-correction, but it was more of a safety feature that did not really get used because the plans and designs were precise and good enough to remove any ambiguity or room for error, and the AI Assistant I used (Claude) was probably very good. This taught me a lot about AI driven development, where the cost of iterations at the build level is correlated with how much uncertainty the build model has, and spending more time planning and designing is often better and cheaper time wise than relying on a self-correcting loop to burn through retries later.
